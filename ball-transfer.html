Machine Design Theory

Foldable Long-Distance Ball Transfer Mechanism

A compact deployable mechanism designed to move a ball between platforms while satisfying strict size, strength, stability, and repeatability constraints.

Project Facts

Role
Team lead, concept designer, fabrication planner
Materials
3D-printed parts, MDF structure, hinges, latch hardware
Main Constraint
Compact size limit with reliable deployment and ball transfer
Outcome
Stable final mechanism with strong final test performance

Design Problem

The mechanism had to pick up, transport, and release a ball from one platform to another while staying within a prescribed volume. I treated the folding behavior as the central design challenge. A mechanism that worked only when already deployed would not be enough; it also had to fold cleanly, deploy repeatedly, and keep enough stiffness after deployment.

Practical Solution

Early folding concepts were not smooth or reliable. I redesigned the fold module around accessible door hinges and latch hardware, then integrated the module into the larger structure. This made the deployment motion more predictable and allowed the team to focus on strengthening the ball-transfer path and improving test consistency.

Engineering Lessons

  • Simple hardware can become a strong design solution when matched carefully to the constraint.
  • Folding mechanisms must be evaluated by both motion quality and locked-state stiffness.
  • Material choice, assembly order, and repair access all affect the final reliability of a student-built machine.
