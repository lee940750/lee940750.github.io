Machine Design Theory

Foldable Long-Distance Ball Transfer Mechanism

A deployable mechanism designed to move a ball between platforms while fitting within strict size limits and staying stiff after deployment.

Role Team lead, concept designer, fabrication planner
Materials 3D-printed parts, MDF structure, hinges, latch hardware
Constraint Compact storage size with reliable deployment and transfer
Result Stable final mechanism with strong final-test performance

Packaging was the core design challenge.

The mechanism had to pick up, transport, and release a ball while staying within a fixed length, width, and height. I treated folding and deployment as primary requirements instead of details to solve after the transfer motion worked.

This changed how we evaluated concepts: a design had to be compact, deploy smoothly, lock into a stiff working state, and remain repairable during repeated testing.

Photo slot Folded mechanism showing compact storage

Simple hardware became the reliable solution.

Early folding concepts were not smooth enough for dependable use. I adapted accessible door hinges and latch hardware into a folding module that gave the structure a clearer motion path and a more predictable locked position.

Fold motion

The mechanism had to move cleanly from storage to working position.

Locked stiffness

The deployed structure needed enough rigidity for repeated ball transfer.

Fabrication

MDF and 3D prints were chosen around available tools and repair speed.

Photo slot Hinge and latch module close-up

Final testing rewarded practical mechanical judgment.

The final design emphasized a buildable, adjustable, and trustworthy mechanism rather than unnecessary complexity. It performed reliably in final evaluation and gave me a stronger sense of how to balance ideal motion with real fabrication constraints.

  • Packaging constraints can dominate mechanism architecture.
  • Off-the-shelf hardware can solve serious design problems when used carefully.
  • Repair access and assembly order matter for final reliability.
