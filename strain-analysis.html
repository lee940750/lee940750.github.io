Spoke Stress Analysis with Strain Gauge and FEA

An experimental and computational workflow for evaluating bicycle spoke stress, strain, yielding, and wheel-level safety factor.

Project Summary

This project investigated how strain gauge measurement and finite element analysis can be used to estimate spoke stress and wheel safety. The goal was to connect theoretical mechanics, experimental measurement, and computational simulation into one validation workflow.

Strain GaugeFEAStress AnalysisSafety FactorValidation

Experimental Design

The proposed experiment uses axial tensile loading on spokes with single-axis strain gauges attached near critical measurement locations. Stepwise loading is used to observe elastic response and identify the onset of plastic deformation through non-recoverable strain behavior.

  • Measurement target: axial strain and corresponding tensile stress.
  • Variables: spoke material and cross-sectional geometry.
  • Failure criterion: yielding indicated by non-recoverable deformation and stress approaching material yield strength.

Finite Element Analysis

A wheel-level simulation was used to estimate stress distribution under hub loading. The model included spoke geometry, pre-tension assumptions, wheel constraints, and increasing vertical load cases to identify regions approaching yield.

The analysis helped visualize how load redistributes through the wheel structure and which spokes become critical under high load.

Discussion

The project emphasizes the relationship between simplified theory and real structural behavior. While axial stress equations provide a first estimate, full wheel behavior depends on geometry, spoke angle, pre-tension, boundary conditions, and load redistribution.
