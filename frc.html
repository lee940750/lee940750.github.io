How I Grew in the Team

I joined the Chien Kuo High School robotics team to learn mechanical design, machining, and programming from senior students. Over time, my role expanded from learning and assisting to leading programming and strategy-related work. This experience was important because it gave me a realistic view of engineering: the robot, software, drivers, scouts, schedule, and competition strategy all affected the final result.

Although my official responsibilities leaned toward programming and strategy, I also participated in early build-season prototyping and mechanism discussions. That mechanical exposure helped me write code with a better understanding of what the robot could physically do and where its limitations came from.

Technical and Strategic Contributions

Autonomous programming

I contributed autonomous-period routines and selected teleoperated logic so the robot could execute competition tasks more reliably.

Scouting app workflow

I led scouting work and helped develop tools for collecting match data quickly during competitions.

Data-driven strategy

I built Excel-based analysis workflows to support alliance selection and tactical decisions under time pressure.

Competition Workflow

  1. During build season, I joined prototype discussions to understand mechanism behavior and translate physical limitations into realistic software expectations.
  2. Before competitions, I prepared autonomous and strategy-related tools so the team could react quickly to different match situations.
  3. During matches, the scouting team collected structured observations instead of relying only on memory or impressions.
  4. Between matches, I used spreadsheets and scouting data to compare teams, identify useful partners, and support strategic decisions.
  5. During alliance selection and match planning, I balanced data, field observations, robot compatibility, and team reliability.
