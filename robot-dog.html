Microprocessor Controlled Systems

Quadruped Robot Dog

A one-semester legged-robot project combining 3D-printed mechanism design, servo control, inverse kinematics, embedded programming, and integration testing.

Role Team lead, mechanical designer, controls developer
Hardware ESP32, PCA9685, eight MG90S servos, 3D-printed structure
Software MATLAB gait simulation and Arduino implementation
Result Walking, standing, sitting, waving, and rocking; course grade A+

Designing a robot that could be repaired, not just demonstrated.

Our team chose a quadruped robot because it forced mechanical design and control logic to be developed together. I designed the body and leg modules with assembly, servo replacement, wire routing, battery placement, and center-of-gravity position in mind.

The goal was a robot that could survive repeated tests. A maintainable structure let us test more aggressively because broken prints, loose wiring, or failed servos could be fixed without rebuilding the entire machine.

From gait simulation to embedded control.

I first tested gait logic and inverse kinematics in MATLAB to make sure the foot trajectory stayed inside the reachable workspace. After the simulated motion was stable, I translated the logic into Arduino code for the ESP32 and PCA9685 servo driver.

Calibration

Each servo direction and angle offset had to match the physical leg geometry.

Gait timing

Foot trajectories, body support, and update timing had to work together.

Output control

The program separated gait update, inverse kinematics, and servo output.

Final behavior and engineering lessons.

The final robot completed forward walking, standing, sitting, waving, and rocking motions. The project showed me how tightly legged robot performance depends on geometry, calibration, structural stiffness, power layout, and timing.

  • Mechanical geometry and control software must be validated together.
  • Repairable hardware makes integration testing much faster.
  • Team leadership matters most when mechanical and software causes overlap.
