Project Summary

This project analyzed a multirotor UAV configuration by connecting component choices to vehicle-level performance. The analysis considered motor KV, battery voltage, ESC current rating, thrust-to-weight ratio, payload margin, and estimated flight time.

MultirotorPropulsionESCBatteryFlight Time

Method

  • Mass budgeting: Estimated aircraft all-up weight and available payload margin.
  • Propulsion estimation: Compared motor and battery configurations using thrust and current limits.
  • Electrical constraints: Checked ESC current rating and battery discharge capability.
  • Flight-time estimate: Related battery capacity, current draw, and usable capacity to endurance.

Engineering Tradeoffs

The main design tradeoff is between thrust margin, endurance, payload capacity, and system weight. A larger battery can increase energy capacity, but also increases takeoff weight and motor load. A higher-voltage configuration may reduce current for the same power level, but requires compatible ESC and motor selection.
